# Summary: Small Actions Make Great Leaders
**Source:** Harvard Business Review (June 2022)
**Author:** Hitendra Wadhwa

### Core Thesis
Leadership is not a set of rare, heroic traits or complex, infrequent behaviors (like "coaching" or "strategic planning"). Instead, it is built from "small actions"—micro-moments lasting 5 to 10 seconds—that are rooted in five core internal energies. By mastering these small building blocks, leadership becomes attainable for everyone in an organization, not just those at the top.

### The Five Core Energies
Great leadership moments emerge when individuals activate five internal "energies":
* **Purpose:** A commitment to a noble, uplifting cause.
* **Wisdom:** A calm, nuanced receptivity to the truth in any situation.
* **Growth:** A constant striving to reach one’s full potential.
* **Love:** Fostering warmth, understanding, and human connection.
* **Self-realization:** Activating the highest human spirit at one's core.

### The Small Actions Framework
The article suggests that complex behaviors are actually combinations of simpler actions. Key examples of these micro-actions include:
* **Disarm:** Affirming a portion of truth in another person's perspective to reduce defensiveness.
* **Appreciate:** Infusing warmth by genuinely valuing the other person’s qualities or efforts.
* **Fuse Opposites:** Acknowledging a flaw while simultaneously highlighting a related strength (e.g., "the writing is subpar, but the research is excellent").
* **Appeal to Values:** Linking a specific task or behavior to a person's core values or a higher impact.
* **Develop a Growth Partnership:** Inviting others to help you grow or suggesting a mutual path for improvement.

### Benefits of the Energy/Action Approach
* **Attainability:** Small actions are easier to learn and practice in the flow of daily work than full-fledged leadership models.
* **Authenticity:** Leaders pick actions that harmonize their inner thoughts and feelings with their outer words, making the interaction feel sincere.
* **Agility:** Because actions are brief, leaders can pivot their approach every few seconds based on the feedback or resistance they receive.

### Conclusion
By shifting the focus from broad competencies to specific, energy-driven actions, organizations can democratize leadership. This "small steps, big leaps" approach allows individuals at all levels to adapt nimbly to disruption and maximize their impact within their sphere of influence.
