# Summary: The Six Signature Traits of Inclusive Leadership (Deloitte)

## Core Concept
Inclusive leadership is a vital capability for navigating a volatile and complex business environment. It requires a shift from the traditional "heroic leader" model to one that emphasizes mindset and behaviors uniquely adapted to diversity.

## The Business Case: Four Global Mega-Trends
Four interrelated shifts are making inclusive leadership a commercial imperative:
* **Diversity of Markets:** Growth is shifting to emerging economies in Asia, Africa, and Latin America, requiring leaders to embed themselves in diverse cultures.
* **Diversity of Customers:** Technology empowers an increasingly diverse customer base that expects personalization, empathy, and connectedness.
* **Diversity of Ideas:** Innovation requires casting a wide net for ideas; diverse-thinking teams protect against groupthink and generate breakthrough insights.
* **Diversity of Talent:** Demographic shifts—including an aging population, the rise of Millennials, and increased migration—demand that leaders optimize a diverse talent pool to ensure equality of opportunity.

## Defining Inclusion
Inclusion is defined by how individuals experience three key elements:
1.  **Fairness and Respect:** Being treated based on unique characteristics rather than stereotypes.
2.  **Value and Belonging:** Feeling that uniqueness is appreciated while maintaining a sense of group membership.
3.  **Confidence and Inspiration:** Having a voice in decision-making and the motivation to perform at one's best.

## The Six Signature Traits and 15 Elements
Inclusive leaders demonstrate six reinforcing traits, comprised of 15 specific elements:

### 1. Commitment
Leaders prioritize diversity and inclusion because it aligns with their personal values and they believe in the business case.
* **Personal Values:** Treating all members with fairness and respect.
* **Business Case Belief:** Treating D&I as a business priority and allocating resources.

### 2. Courage
Leaders challenge the status quo and are humble about their own limitations.
* **Humility:** Acknowledging personal strengths/weaknesses and admitting mistakes.
* **Bravery:** Acting as an agent for change and holding others accountable.

### 3. Cognizance of Bias
Leaders are mindful of personal and organizational blind spots and self-regulate to ensure fair play.
* **Self-regulation:** Developing processes to mitigate unconscious bias.
* **Fair Play:** Awareness of subtle biases like similarity-attraction and in-group favoritism.

### 4. Curiosity
Leaders possess an open mindset and a deep desire to understand others without judgment.
* **Openness:** Seeking different ideas and experiences to enable growth.
* **Perspective-taking:** Actively seeking to understand the world through different cultural frames.
* **Coping with Ambiguity:** Navigating the uncertainty inherent in diverse environments.

### 5. Cultural Intelligence
Leaders function effectively in different cultural settings and adapt as required.
* **Drive:** The motivation to learn about other cultures.
* **Knowledge:** Understanding cultural norms and differences.
* **Adaptability:** Changing behavior to suit different cultural contexts.

### 6. Collaboration
Leaders empower diverse-thinking teams to ensure the whole is greater than the sum of its parts.
* **Empowerment:** Enabling direct reports to develop and excel.
* **Teaming:** Building shared aspiration and aligned understanding.
* **Voice:** Ensuring all team members feel confident to speak up.
